Marketing Strategy
Step 1 - Add Your Keywords to KDP

If you have not already done so, adding the right keywords to your KDP listing is one of the highest-impact actions you can take. Amazon uses your keywords to decide which searches your book appears in. Without them, your book is invisible to buyers who are actively looking for what you have written.

✓
Use all 7 keyword fields
KDP gives you 7 keyword fields, each supporting up to 50 characters. Use every one. Think about how your reader searches, not how you would describe your book. Phrases like “dog grooming business book” or “how to start a mobile dog grooming business” are more valuable than broad single words.
✓
What not to do
Do not repeat words already in your title or subtitle. Do not use competitor names, celebrity names, or misleading terms. Amazon has tightened its algorithm and listings with irrelevant or manipulative keywords risk being suppressed. Keep them accurate and specific.
✓
Use the recommended keywords in this audit
The keywords section of this audit contains research-backed terms for your niche. These have been selected based on search volume, relevance, and competition level. Copy them directly into your KDP keyword fields.
↓
Step 2 - Analyse Your Competing Books

Before promoting your book, understand what you are up against. The best-selling books in the pet business and dog grooming space are bestsellers for a reason. Study them and use what you learn to make your own listing, content, and positioning stronger.

✓
Study their covers, titles and descriptions
Look at the top 5 to 10 competing books. What do their covers have in common? How are their titles worded? What promises do their descriptions make? These choices are not accidental. Model what is working.
✓
Read their reviews carefully
Positive reviews tell you what buyers value most. Critical reviews tell you what is missing. If multiple readers say a competing book lacks specific pricing guidance for mobile setups, that is a gap you can fill. Look for patterns, not individual opinions.
✓
Buy the top performers if you can
Reading the competition is an investment, not an expense. Look at their sample pages, table of contents, and structure. Understanding why they sell is the fastest way to produce something that competes with them or stands clearly apart.
↓
Step 3 - Get Some Ratings and Reviews

Amazon distinguishes between ratings and reviews. A rating is simply a star score with no written comment, while a review includes written feedback alongside the stars. Both count towards your overall star rating and help your book appear credible to buyers, but written reviews carry more weight because they give shoppers context and confidence.

Before you start promoting, building up a mix of both is one of the most valuable things you can do.

✓
Ask for a rating or review inside your book
Add a short, genuine request at the back of your book asking readers to leave a rating or review if they found it useful. Also add one in the middle of the book as many readers never reach the final pages. Keep it natural and never ask for a 5-star review specifically as Amazon takes a dim view of this and readers find it off-putting.
✓
Aim for 10 to 15 ratings and reviews before promoting
Social proof is the single most important factor in converting browsers into buyers on Amazon. Aim for 10 to 15 combined ratings and reviews before you expect consistent organic sales. Do not launch Amazon Ads until you have at least 5 as a listing without social proof will waste your budget.
✓
Use an ARC service to reach readers before launch
ARC (Advance Review Copy) services such as BookSirens and Hidden Gems connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. These services are legitimate, widely used, and fully compliant with Amazon’s terms of service. They are one of the most effective ways to build early social proof. Do not pay anyone to leave a positive review and do not use services that guarantee a specific star rating.
✓
Promote to communities where your readers already gather
For a book aimed at aspiring dog groomers and pet business owners, there are active communities on Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok where your target readers already gather. Groups for dog groomers, mobile pet business owners, and pet care entrepreneurs are a natural fit. Share a practical pricing tip or business insight from the book rather than leading with a sales pitch. Demonstrating genuine expertise builds trust and makes readers far more likely to buy and review.

For publishing support and strategy, communities like the 20Booksto50K Facebook group and the Alliance of Independent Authors are excellent free resources used by professional self-publishers worldwide.
↓
Step 4 - Publish a Hardback Edition

You already have Kindle and paperback formats live, which is a strong start. Publishing a hardback edition is the move that unlocks the next level of revenue. In the pet business category, the top-earning titles carry more authority in hardback, and physical books dominate the revenue chart.

✓
Price it correctly to make it worthwhile
KDP Print books have a minimum list price based on page count and trim size. The royalty is 60% of the list price minus the printing cost. To clear at least $5 per copy, the book needs to be priced high enough to cover KDP’s printing fee and still leave that margin on top. In a professional niche where buyers expect to pay for specialist content, a higher price point is entirely justified.
✓
Pet business guides are reference books. Groomers want them in print
Dog groomers and mobile pet business owners use guides as references. A physical hardback sits on a shelf, gets flipped to mid-appointment, and gets recommended to colleagues. A Kindle edition does not serve that use case in the same way.
✓
Hardbacks perform better in Amazon Ads
Sponsored Products campaigns for physical books have higher click-through and conversion rates in business guide categories. The book cover is the ad. A hardback carries far more visual weight in search results than a Kindle thumbnail.
✓
Both formats appear on the same listing
Once the hardback is live, Kindle, paperback, and hardback all appear on the same Amazon product page. Buyers choose their preferred format, and you earn from all three.

Step 6 - Amazon Advertising

Once you have reviews and confidence in your listing, Amazon Sponsored Products advertising can become one of the most effective revenue channels available to a self-published author. The principle is straightforward: you invest a controlled daily budget, and if your campaign is optimised correctly, every dollar you spend returns more than a dollar in royalties.

Unlike most forms of marketing, Amazon Ads are directly measurable. You can see exactly what you are spending, what is converting, and what your return is in real time. This means you can forecast your sales with a level of precision that most businesses can only dream of.

There is real risk in getting it wrong, so seek support if you are unsure. Start with a budget of $5 to $10 per day, test the data, and only scale what is demonstrably working.

✓
Understand your ACoS
ACoS stands for Advertising Cost of Sale, the percentage of each sale that goes towards your ad spend. If your book earns $5 in royalties and you spend $2 on ads to make that sale, your ACoS is 40%. A target ACoS of 30 to 40% is a solid benchmark for KDP books.
✓
Optimise before you scale
Pause keywords that spend without converting. Lower bids on high-ACoS terms. Increase bids on what is working. Do not increase your budget until your ACoS is consistently within your target range.
✓
Scale what works
Once you find a profitable ACoS that holds consistently, increase your daily budget with confidence. Every dollar you put in returns more than a dollar in royalties. The ceiling is not your budget. It is the total demand for books in your niche.

The Log Book Space: Fast to Publish, Consistent Monthly Income
Dog grooming appointment books and client log books are actively searched on Amazon, and most of what is currently available is generic, low-quality, and written by people who have never set foot in a grooming salon. An author with 10 years of experience knows exactly what a groomer needs to track. That knowledge advantage is the product.
✓
Low-content, high-margin, quick to produce
A dog grooming client record book (breed notes, coat type, service history, pricing, appointment slots) can be designed and live on Amazon within days. It requires no writing, just smart formatting from someone who understands the workflow.
✓
Publish multiple variants to multiply listings
Dog grooming client log · Grooming appointment planner · Pet salon job tracker. Each targets a slightly different keyword and appears as a separate listing. A catalogue of three log books could generate £500–£1,500/mo combined with minimal ongoing effort.
✓
The main book and log books sell to the same buyer
Someone who buys the Blueprint to start their business will also want a client log book to run it. Amazon’s “customers also bought” algorithm will surface both titles together, compounding visibility across the catalogue.
